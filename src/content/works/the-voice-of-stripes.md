textEn: "The voice of stripes is a sound installation created in collaboration with Jinju Ma. It was presented in The Poetics of Knowledge: The Sound of Fashion, a Fashion and Textiles Research exhibition at the Royal College of Art (RCA). The work approaches stripes as a visual and sonic notation: their intervals, repetitions, rhythm, movement, and proportion suggest ways of hearing, turning a printed surface into a sound situation in which looking and listening continuously overlap.\n\nA stripe begins with a line, and a line with a point that leaves a trace. As these traces accumulate through movement and experience, each person makes a pattern that cannot be repeated by another. The work approaches stripes as a graphic score and an algorithmic composition: a record of direction, difference, and personality.\n\nA fixed line can appear to move as a listener changes position, much like a musical phrase unfolds over time. A vibration speaker lets the printed polyvinyl display act as an ambient sound source, while an ultrasonic speaker creates a focused listening point. Moving between these two conditions, the audience experiences the garment and composition as connected performances."

Each stripe is read as a set of data — interval, density, direction, duration, and repetition — then passed through an algorithmic composition system to generate a corresponding sonic event.
